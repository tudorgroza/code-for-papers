- stage: PRENATAL
  phenotypes: 
    - Polyhydramnios
    - Increased nuchal translucency
    - Fetal hydrops
    - Congenital heart defects (e.g., pulmonary valve stenosis)
    - Cystic hygroma

- stage: CONGENITAL
  phenotypes: 
    - Congenital heart defects (e.g., pulmonary valve stenosis)
    - Hypertelorism (wide-spaced eyes)
    - Cryptorchidism (in males)
    - Webbed neck
    - Low-set, posteriorly rotated ears
    - Short stature (birth length below average)
    - Broad or webbed neck
    - Lymphedema (hands and feet)
    - Ptosis (droopy eyelids)
    - Chest deformities (pectus excavatum or carinatum)

- stage: NEONATAL
  phenotypes: 
    - Feeding difficulties (due to hypotonia)
    - Hypotonia (low muscle tone)
    - Short stature (below average length at birth)
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Jaundice (prolonged neonatal jaundice) 
    - Dysmorphic facial features (wide-spaced eyes, low-set ears)
    - Pulmonary valve stenosis

- stage: INFANTILE
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Feeding difficulties
    - Developmental delay (mild to moderate)
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Dysmorphic facial features (broad forehead, down-slanting eyes)
    - Chest deformities (pectus excavatum or carinatum)
    - Hypotonia
    - Cryptorchidism (in males)
    - Joint hypermobility

- stage: CHILDHOOD
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Developmental delay (mild to moderate)
    - Learning disabilities
    - Speech delays
    - Mild intellectual disability
    - Joint hypermobility
    - Scoliosis
    - Cardiac abnormalities
    - Failure to thrive (in early childhood)
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds) 
    - Strabismus (crossed eyes)
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)

- stage: JUVENILE
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Learning disabilities 
    - Mild intellectual disability
    - Pubertal delay
    - Cryptorchidism (in males)
    - Joint hypermobility
    - Scoliosis
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds)
    - Strabismus (crossed eyes)
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)

- stage: EARLY ADULT
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Mild intellectual disability
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Cryptorchidism (in males)
    - Scoliosis
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds)

- stage: INTERMEDIATE EARLY ADULT
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Joint hypermobility
    - Scoliosis
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds)

- stage: LATE EARLY ADULT
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Scoliosis
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds)
    - Joint hypermobility
    - Fertility issues in males (due to cryptorchidism)

- stage: MIDDLE AGE
  phenotypes: 
    - Short stature
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)
    - Increased risk of neoplasms (e.g., leukemia, lymphoma)
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds)
    - Joint pain/osteoarthritis
    - Scoliosis

- stage: LATE
  phenotypes: 
    - Cardiac abnormalities (e.g., hypertrophic cardiomyopathy)
    - Hearing loss (sensorineural or conductive)
    - Increased risk of neoplasms (e.g., leukemia, lymphoma)
    - Cognitive decline/dementia
    - Bleeding disorders (easy bruising, nosebleeds)
    - Joint pain/osteoarthritis
